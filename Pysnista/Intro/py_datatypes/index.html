Sets

Sets are unordered, which means that they can‚Äôt be indexed.
They cannot contain duplicate elements.

Due to the way they‚Äôre stored, it‚Äôs faster to check whether an item is part of a set, rather than part of a list.

Membership testing and the elimination of duplicate entries

  • The union operator | combines two sets to form a new one containing items in either.
  • The intersection operator & gets items only in both.
  • The difference operator - gets items in the first set but not in the second.
  • The symmetric_difference operator ^ gets items in either set, but not both.
